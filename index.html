• 1. Primary
    Linear chain of Amino Acids.
    2. Secondary
    alpha or beta regions stabilized by hydrogen bonds between atoms of the polypeptide backbone.
    Note: Backbone, not AA.
    3. Tertiary
    3-D shape stabilized by interactions between R-Groups (side chains).
    Note: this is the polypeptide's overall shape.
    4. Quaternary
    Several Polypeptides (3o)forming the functional Protein

  • R-NH2- + R-COOH- ⇆ R-NH-CO-R + H2O

    Peptide bonds are formed via condensation reactions

    There are only 2 terminus' so the chemical nature is dictated by the R-groups, not the polar ends.

  • Shape determines the function of the cell.Most notably, shape is a spontaneous process, from the interactions of the 2o and 3o subunits, which are formed by the 1o structure because of the Amino Acids.Folding into its 3-D structure begins as the protein is being synthesized.More advanced, there are 3 factors affecting cell structure as well.⇒pH ⇒Temperature ⇒Salt concentration ⇒Other Environmental factorsAltering one of these factors leads to denaturation. This is the loss of the proteins shape rendering the protein inactive.
  • Raising temperature agitates the polypeptide chain enough to overcome the weak interactions that stabilize the structure.
    Blood is a Weak Base (7.4) and stomach acid is a Strong Acid (2). If blood or the stomach are outside of these ranges, the proteins that handle metabolizm and catabolism will denature.
